This week's incidents reveal operational gaps at the vendor contract level. Two districts are auditing photography vendor data practices after community concerns about FERPA compliance. Incidents
1
California releases AI safeguards after Adobe generated inappropriate images for 4th grade project
CalMatters · February 26, 2026
What happened
Adobe's AI image generator produced sexualized images when a 4th grade student entered prompts for a book project. The incident coincided with California's release of new statewide AI safety guidelines for schools.
Who's affected
All California districts using Adobe Creative Cloud or other generative AI image tools for student projects.
Compliance Exposure
If the tool was approved without content filtering review, the district may lack documentation showing it vetted AI outputs for age-appropriateness. Federal monitors in Title IX or child safety audits would look for: approval records showing content moderation was assessed, incident logs documenting how the exposure was handled, and parent notification records.
Recommended Action
Pull approval records for all AI image generation tools currently in use. If content filtering was not documented during vetting, add a supplemental review note now. Review California's new guidelines and compare to your current AI approval workflow.
Workflow Impact
Vendor Management: Add content moderation review to AI tool approval checklist.
2
Two districts reviewing photography vendor agreements after community concerns about student photo data use
KETK / IV Press Online · March 2026
What happened
Kilgore ISD (Texas) and El Centro Elementary School District (California) are both responding to parent concerns that Lifetouch, their school photography vendor, may be using student photos or information for non-educational purposes. Both districts are now auditing their vendor data agreements for FERPA compliance.
Who's affected
All districts contracting with photography vendors who receive student names, images, or directory information.
Compliance Exposure
If the vendor agreement does not explicitly limit use of student photos and directory information to the school's direct educational purpose, the district may have an unauthorized disclosure under FERPA. Federal auditors would look for: a signed BAA or data sharing agreement restricting use, a log showing the contract was reviewed before directory information was shared, and evidence the district designated directory information in its annual notice.
Recommended Action
Pull your current photography vendor contract. Confirm it includes language restricting the vendor from using student data, including photos, for marketing or non-school purposes. If the restriction is missing, request an amendment this week. Document the review in your vendor compliance log.
Workflow Impact
Student Data Privacy: Add photo vendor agreements to annual FERPA contract review cycle.
3
Texas district pilots ChatGPT for teachers but has not clarified data training terms
MSN / Humble ISD · March 2026
What happened
Humble ISD (Texas) has signed an agreement to pilot OpenAI's ChatGPT for teacher use. The district has not yet clarified whether teacher prompts, lesson plans, or other inputs will be used to train OpenAI's models.
Who's affected
All districts piloting or contracting with OpenAI, Microsoft Copilot, or other generative AI platforms for staff use.
Compliance Exposure
If teachers enter student names, IEP details, behavior notes, or other education records into the tool and the data is used for training, the district has an unauthorized FERPA disclosure. If the agreement lacks an opt-out from training, the district has no documentation showing it protected student information. Auditors would look for: a signed data processing agreement specifying no training use, staff training logs showing teachers were told what data may not be entered, and a list of approved use cases.
Recommended Action
If your district is piloting any AI tool for staff, confirm your agreement includes an explicit opt-out from model training. If teachers are already using the tool, issue interim guidance this week: do not enter student names, IEP content, discipline records, or any FERPA-protected information until the training opt-out is confirmed in writing.
Workflow Impact
Compliance & Reporting: Add AI model training terms to vendor contract checklist for all generative AI tools.
